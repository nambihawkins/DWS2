Birth

Aiden, you were born January 24, 2015 at Southern Maryland Hospital Center at 5:53pm weighing 6lb 5oz. If your mommy had been induced on the 26th (Monday) instead of the 23rd (Friday), you would have been born on my birthday. There were tons of people at the hospital awaiting your arrival! I was in the delivery room with your mommy and I had the most important role of holding her foot while you made your way into this world!

When mommy first saw you, she says,

"I cried so hard, you were so precious. I kept saying, 'My baby, that’s my baby.' I couldn’t stop crying. One of my most memorable moments was when the doctors had to take you and I yelled from across the room, 'Does he have 5 fingers and toes!?'"

When Daddy first saw you, he says,

"I saw you and I tried not to cry, but when I saw mommy cry, I hugged her and cried. I was so happy to see my baby boy."

One of my most memorable moments was when your mommy was learning how to swaddle you and on the first try she cried because she couldn’t do it right (lol!). I guess it was that post-partum depression and what not. Now looking back at it she can laugh.

The Great(est) Grands

So Aiden, you have some very great grandparents. I wouldn’t go as far to say that they are the "Greatest on Earth"… BUT bet you won’t ever hear of anyone greater than them. You know them as GiGi and Pop Pop, but I know them as grandma and Daddy. They helped raise your mommy and me, and now they’re helping to raise you. I like to say that the more children you have, the greater you become. When you came into the world Grandma and Daddy went from just grandparents to GREAT-grandparents. They make sure you have all that you need and then some… and then more!

Though taking care of a little one can get very tiresome, Pop Pop and GiGi make it look so easy. Sometimes I think their true age should be mine. I’ll come home from work all kinds of worn out and tired, so will your mommy, but then we’ll see Pop Pop and Gigi playing with you, still full of energy after playing with you all day long. Don’t ask me how they do it, and how they’ve done it for 3 generations, because I don’t know. Just know that they are doing it and they’re doing an excellent job.
